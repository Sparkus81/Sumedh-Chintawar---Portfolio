Formula SAE Pedal Box Heel Stop

HYTECH Electric Racing | CATIA V5, FEA | 2025
CATIA FEA Structural Analysis 3D Printing Weight Optimization Automotive

Project Overview

Designed and fabricated a high-performance, load-bearing heel stop component for an electric Formula SAE race car's pedal box assembly. The project required achieving optimal structural integrity while minimizing weight and improving driver ergonomics under rigorous competition constraints.

Design Images

3D printed model with heel shape and size testing
Pedal Box Component setup with heel stop installed
V0 of Heel Stop
V1 of Heel Stop

Technical Approach

  • Structural Design: Engineered in CATIA with iterative FEA validation to ensure component could withstand 200+ lb pedal forces while meeting FSAE safety requirements
  • Weight Optimization: Achieved 35% weight reduction through topology optimization and strategic material removal while maintaining structural integrity
  • Material Selection: Specified high-grade PLA carbon fiber hybrid for optimal strength-to-weight ratio and rapid prototyping capability
  • Manufacturing Process: Prototyped using FDM 3D printing for rapid iteration, validating fit and ergonomics with drivers before finalizing design
  • Analysis & Validation: Performed comprehensive FEA to validate stress distributions and safety factors, followed by physical load testing to verify analytical predictions
Software Used: CATIA V5, FEA Tools
Design Load: 200+ lbs pedal force
Material: High-grade PLA carbon fiber hybrid
Weight Reduction: 35% vs. previous design
Manufacturing: FDM 3D printing with optimized infill

Results & Impact

Successfully reduced component mass by 35% while improving driver comfort and maintaining all FSAE safety requirements. Component passed technical inspections and performed reliably throughout the 2024-2025 racing season with zero failures.

Hydrophobic Umbrella Design

ME 3210 Design & Manufacturing | SolidWorks | Fall 2024
SolidWorks Product Design Materials Engineering Manufacturing Processes Mechanism Design

Project Overview

Designed an innovative umbrella incorporating hydrophobic nanomaterial coating and low-friction mechanisms to address common issues with traditional umbrellas. The design focused on water repellency, improved folding mechanisms, and user convenience while considering manufacturing feasibility and cost-effectiveness.

Design Images

Complete umbrella assembly showing canopy and frame structure
Detailed view of mechanism and structural components
Frame assembly highlighting hinge design

Problem Statement

  • Traditional umbrellas retain water after use, requiring umbrella bags and causing water drip on users and belongings
  • Folding mechanisms frequently jam due to water buildup in hinges and joints
  • Poor water management reduces durability and user experience

Design Solutions

  • Hydrophobic Nanomaterial Coating: Applied titanium dioxide (TiO₂) nano-coating to canopy fabric using spray deposition method, creating superhydrophobic surface that actively repels water droplets
  • Low-Friction Guide Rails: Engineered sliding mechanism using low-friction plastic materials to prevent water-induced jamming and ensure smooth operation
  • Enhanced Hinge Design: Redesigned hinge assemblies with water-resistant properties and improved clearances to maintain functionality in wet conditions
  • Material Selection: Specified aluminum alloy frame for strength-to-weight optimization and corrosion resistance in wet environments

Manufacturing Processes

  • Nano Spray Deposition: Applied hydrophobic coating through controlled spray process for uniform nanomaterial distribution across canopy surface
  • Plastic Injection Molding: High-consistency injection molding for low-friction plastic components ensuring dimensional accuracy and repeatability
  • Aluminum Forming: Precision forming and joining of aluminum alloy structural frame components
Software Used: SolidWorks 2024
Project Type: ME 3210 Design & Manufacturing
Key Innovation: Hydrophobic nano-coating + low-friction mechanisms
Materials: Titanium Dioxide (TiO₂), Aluminum Alloy, Low-friction Plastics
Manufacturing: Nano spray deposition, injection molding, aluminum forming

Design Trade-offs

Advantages: Superior water repellency, enhanced ease of use, improved durability and robustness of folding mechanism, better user experience in wet conditions.

Challenges: Increased manufacturing complexity requiring specialized nano-coating equipment, higher production cost compared to traditional umbrellas, need for quality control in nano-deposition process.

Key Takeaways

  • Gained experience integrating advanced materials (nanomaterials) into consumer product design
  • Learned to balance innovation with manufacturing feasibility and cost constraints
  • Developed understanding of surface engineering and hydrophobic coating applications
  • Practiced selecting appropriate manufacturing processes for novel material systems
  • Improved skills in mechanism design for reliability in challenging environmental conditions
