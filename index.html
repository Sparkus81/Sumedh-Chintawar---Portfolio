Formula SAE Pedal Box Heel Stop

HYTECH Electric Racing | CATIA V5, FEA | 2025
CATIA FEA Structural Analysis 3D Printing Weight Optimization Automotive

Project Overview

Designed and fabricated a high-performance, load-bearing heel stop component for an electric Formula SAE race car's pedal box assembly. The project required achieving optimal structural integrity while minimizing weight and improving driver ergonomics under rigorous competition constraints.

Design Images

3D printed model with heel shape and size testing
Pedal Box Component setup with heel stop installed
V0 of Heel Stop
V1 of Heel Stop

Technical Approach

  • Structural Design: Engineered in CATIA with iterative FEA validation to ensure component could withstand 200+ lb pedal forces while meeting FSAE safety requirements
  • Weight Optimization: Achieved 35% weight reduction through topology optimization and strategic material removal while maintaining structural integrity
  • Material Selection: Specified high-grade PLA carbon fiber hybrid for optimal strength-to-weight ratio and rapid prototyping capability
  • Manufacturing Process: Prototyped using FDM 3D printing for rapid iteration, validating fit and ergonomics with drivers before finalizing design
  • Analysis & Validation: Performed comprehensive FEA to validate stress distributions and safety factors, followed by physical load testing to verify analytical predictions
Software Used: CATIA V5, FEA Tools
Design Load: 200+ lbs pedal force
Material: High-grade PLA carbon fiber hybrid
Weight Reduction: 35% vs. previous design
Manufacturing: FDM 3D printing with optimized infill

Results & Impact

Successfully reduced component mass by 35% while improving driver comfort and maintaining all FSAE safety requirements. Component passed technical inspections and performed reliably throughout the 2024-2025 racing season with zero failures.

Mario Kart Blue Shell

Personal Project | SolidWorks | 2024
SolidWorks Surface Modeling Organic Shapes Assembly Design Rendering

Project Overview

Recreation of the iconic Blue Shell from Mario Kart using advanced surface modeling techniques in SolidWorks. This project challenged proficiency in creating complex organic shapes while maintaining clean surface continuity and proper design intent for 3D printing manufacturability.

Design Images

Wing assembly with mounting features
Shell base with internal structure
Complete assembly with rendered shadows
Shell body showing surface detail and spikes

Technical Approach

  • Surface Modeling: Utilized lofted surfaces, boundary surfaces, and swept features to create smooth organic curves matching the iconic Blue Shell profile
  • Feature Management: Maintained organized feature tree with design intent for easy modifications and parametric updates
  • Assembly Design: Created multi-body parts and sub-assemblies for the shell body, wings, and detail features with proper mate constraints
  • DFM Considerations: Designed with 3D printing in mind, including proper draft angles, wall thicknesses (minimum 2mm), and support-minimizing orientation
  • Detail Features: Incorporated characteristic spikes, panel lines, and geometric details while maintaining clean topology
Software Used: SolidWorks 2024
Primary Techniques: Surface modeling, lofts, boundary surfaces
Part Count: 4 unique components (shell body, base, 2x wings)
File Format: SLDPRT, STL export for 3D printing
Manufacturing: FDM 3D printing (PLA/ABS)

Key Takeaways
